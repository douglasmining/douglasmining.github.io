Water, mineral deposits, caves or tunnels, archaeological sites, and even buried caches can be meaningfully identified using this tool.

 The Basic Principals of Resistivity:

A Resistivity Survey is conducted by placing metal probes into the ground and an electrical current is passed between them. The resistance of the current is measured in Ohms. Depending on the resistance the current encounters, you can identify different underground anomalies. A high resistance anomaly would indicate something like a void, cave, or tunnel. A low resistance anomaly would indicate something like metal, minerals, or water. 

Imaging Basics:

In order to create an Image with the device, 8 to 16 probes are inserted into the ground and all possible combinations are scanned. The device guides you through this process and automatically creates an image of what is underground.
